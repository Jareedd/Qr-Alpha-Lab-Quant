# Fresh edge ideation — post trial #11 (2026-06-15)

**Status: zero-trial.** No real price data touched; N unchanged (= 11). A
`/goal` ideation pass after H6 (trial #11) died on implementability — to ask,
honestly, whether any *new* free-data hypothesis has a genuine shot at
graduating, or whether the funnel is dry and the real unlock is data (CRSP).

## The screens (a candidate must survive all five)

1. **Survivorship.** Lives where nothing dies, dies conservatively (bias
   provably against us), or is collection-forward. (Cost trials #1–7 their alpha;
   the dead-fund census is what saved H6's *direction*.)
2. **Borrow.** No reliance on shorting hard-to-borrow names — the recurring
   assassin on every short leg — or a declared long-tilt fallback.
3. **Cost mortality.** Turnover low enough that net ≈ gross at retail costs
   (Novy-Marx–Velikov; it killed trials #2–7).
4. **Implementability (NEW, the H6 lesson).** The edge must survive a one-period
   entry lag — no shared-price / bid-ask-bounce reversal masquerading as signal.
5. **Capacity-honest.** A personal-book edge is fine; say the number plainly.

## Candidates screened

| idea | verdict | why |
|---|---|---|
| **Post-earnings-announcement drift (PEAD)** | **PARK** | Among the most persistent published anomalies; PIT S&P universe already built. But free earnings-*surprise* data is thin/unreliable, and the profitable leg leans short (borrow). Data-gated like H1 — a candidate **only if** a clean earnings-surprise feed appears (WRDS/IBES would supply it). |
| **Volatility risk premium (systematic short vol)** | **KILL (free data)** | A genuinely structural premium — but needs clean options history (not free), and it is *crash-skewed* (the −1.87-skew assassin that capped carry on the skew-aware DSR). Same failure mode as #8, without #8's free data. |
| **Crypto cash-and-carry basis (dated futures vs spot)** | **PARK** | Real structural carry, survivorship-OK (exchange dumps). But thin and heavily arbitraged for retail, and a near-cousin of the perp carry already shown to decay (#8/#10). Worth a fee-first power check before anything else; low priority. |
| **Crypto spot cross-sectional momentum/reversal** | **KILL** | Picked-over and decayed; no survivorship moat beyond the majors; reversal variants risk the exact #11 microstructure-bounce trap. |
| **Index *addition* (reconstitution) effect** | **KILL** | The mirror of H8's deletion null — arbitraged, borrow-dependent, and decayed to ~0 (Greenwood–Sammon). #9 already closed this family. |
| **Lottery / MAX (short high-skew names)** | **KILL** | The edge lives on the *short* leg of unborrowable names — the assassin by construction; the long-only tilt loses it. |
| **Equity pairs / cointegration stat-arb** | **KILL** | High microstructure / entry-lag exposure — precisely the #11 bounce trap — plus low capacity and heavy arbitrage. We just learned what this looks like. |
| **Turn-of-month / holiday seasonality** | **KILL** | Weak prior, picked over, almost certainly noise at honest N. |

## Verdict

**No high-odds free-data candidate survives the full screen.** The two least-bad
(PEAD, crypto basis) are *parked-pending* — both data-gated or thin, neither a
strong graduation bet. This is not a dead end to hide; it is the project's
central finding, reproduced one more time at the ideation stage: **free-data
cross-sectional alpha, honestly screened, is largely exhausted.**

The genuine path to a graduation therefore runs through **better data, not more
free-data trials** — specifically **CRSP → H1 (fundamental quality)**: the lowest
DSR hurdle on the board (15-yr sample, slow turnover, benign skew) and the one
whose survivorship hole CRSP directly closes. PEAD joins the same data-gated
queue (IBES earnings surprises).

**Recommendation:** CRSP→H1 primary; PEAD and crypto-basis as parked backups
contingent on a data source; build the risk/execution engine in parallel as the
honest "scale it" infrastructure for whatever eventually graduates. Funnel is
thin by honest measurement — and that, documented, is itself a result.
